\subsubsection{\texorpdfstring{$M_8$}{M_8} Overview}
\label{sec:m8-overview}

$M_8$ is the meta-space enabling the existence of 
civilizational-technological continua ($K_8$). 
While $M_7$ supports social coordination through shared meaning, norms, and 
communication, $M_8$ introduces the structural, symbolic, and material 
conditions for:

\begin{itemize}
    \item externalized memory (writing, notation, symbolic media),
    \item technological infrastructures,
    \item institutional persistence across generations,
    \item distributed economic and informational flows,
    \item large-scale collective organization,
    \item long-range stabilization of social and symbolic cycles.
\end{itemize}

Thus $M_8$ is the minimal space in which \emph{civilization} becomes an admissible continuum.

% ---------------------------------------------------------------
\subsubsection*{1. Admissible Configuration Space \texorpdfstring{$\Omega(M_8)$}{\Omega(M_8)}}

\[
\Omega(M_8)=
\left\{
(
\mathcal{S},\
\mathcal{I},\
A_{\mathrm{symbol}},\
A_{\mathrm{tech}},\
\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{infra}},\
C_{\mathrm{civ}},\
\Sigma_8
)
\mid \text{admissibility conditions hold}
\right\}.
\]

Components:

\begin{itemize}
    \item $\mathcal{S}$ — stable symbolic systems (writing, mathematics, codified knowledge),
    \item $\mathcal{I}$ — institutional structures (law, governance, economic systems),
    \item $A_{\mathrm{symbol}}$ — axes of symbolic representation,
    \item $A_{\mathrm{tech}}$ — axes for technological differentiation,
    \item $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{infra}}$ — material and informational infrastructures,
    \item $C_{\mathrm{civ}}$ — civilizational cycles (production–distribution–feedback),
    \item $\Sigma_8$ — global constraints ensuring long-term stability.
\end{itemize}

Admissibility requires:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item symbolic stability: reproducibility of symbols across time,
    \item institutional persistence: $\tau_{\mathrm{inst}} \gg \tau_{\mathrm{social}}$,
    \item infrastructural capacity supporting large-scale flows,
    \item boundedness of civilizational tension $T_8$,
    \item non-emptiness of $\Omega(K_8)$ under $M_8$ constraints.
\end{enumerate}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------
\subsubsection*{2. Admissible Axes \texorpdfstring{$A(M_8)$}{A(M_8)}}

\[
A(M_8)=
\{
A_{\mathrm{symbol}},\
A_{\mathrm{tech}},\
A_{\mathrm{infrastructure}},\
A_{\mathrm{institution}},\
A_{\mathrm{economic}},\
A_{\mathrm{scientific}},\
A_{\mathrm{bureaucratic}}
\}.
\]

Descriptions:

\begin{itemize}
    \item $A_{\mathrm{symbol}}$ — discrete symbolic distinctions (script, notation, code),
    \item $A_{\mathrm{tech}}$ — technological differentiation (tools, machines, platforms),
    \item $A_{\mathrm{infrastructure}}$ — transport, communication, energy networks,
    \item $A_{\mathrm{institution}}$ — legal and governance axes,
    \item $A_{\mathrm{economic}}$ — value, exchange, resource allocation axes,
    \item $A_{\mathrm{scientific}}$ — axes of theoretical and empirical representation,
    \item $A_{\mathrm{bureaucratic}}$ — formal procedural distinctions.
\end{itemize}

Necessary condition for civilization:

\[
\dim(A_{\mathrm{symbol}}) \ge 1
\quad\text{and}\quad
A_{\mathrm{tech}}\neq\emptyset.
\]

\subsubsection*{3. Potentials \texorpdfstring{$P(M_8)$}{P(M_8)}}

Civilizational potentials extend social potentials with symbolic and material components:

\[
P(M_8)=
(F_{\mathrm{symbol}},\
F_{\mathrm{tech}},\
F_{\mathrm{infrastructure}},\
F_{\mathrm{institution}},\
F_{\mathrm{economic}},\
F_{\mathrm{scientific}},\
F_{\mathrm{stability}}).
\]

Interpretation:

\begin{itemize}
    \item $F_{\mathrm{symbol}}$ — potential enabling encoding and transmission of abstract distinctions,
    \item $F_{\mathrm{tech}}$ — gradients enabling technological innovation and adoption,
    \item $F_{\mathrm{infrastructure}}$ — potentials enabling transport, energy, communication flows,
    \item $F_{\mathrm{institution}}$ — regulation and constraint potentials,
    \item $F_{\mathrm{economic}}$ — production/exchange potentials,
    \item $F_{\mathrm{scientific}}$ — epistemic potential for stable theoretical growth,
    \item $F_{\mathrm{stability}}$ — long-range coherence potential stabilizing $K_8$.
\end{itemize}

Civilization exists when:

\[
F_{\mathrm{symbol}} > \Theta_{\mathrm{entropy}},
\quad
F_{\mathrm{institution}} > \Theta_{\mathrm{collapse}}.
\]

% ---------------------------------------------------------------
\subsubsection*{4. Thresholds \texorpdfstring{$\Theta(M_8)$}{\Theta(M_8)}}

Key thresholds:

\begin{itemize}
    \item $\Theta_{\mathrm{symbol}}$ — minimal reliability of symbolic reproduction,
    \item $\Theta_{\mathrm{infrastructure}}$ — minimal infrastructural capacity,
    \item $\Theta_{\mathrm{institution}}$ — critical strength of institutions,
    \item $\Theta_{\mathrm{economic}}$ — viability threshold for resource circulation,
    \item $\Theta_{\mathrm{scientific}}$ — threshold for stable knowledge systems,
    \item $\Theta_{\mathrm{entropy}}$ — maximal entropy of communication tolerated,
    \item $\Theta_{\mathrm{collapse}}$ — boundary associated with civilizational failure.
\end{itemize}

Crossing $\Theta_{\mathrm{collapse}}$ induces breakdown into fragmented $K_7$ continua.

J(M_8)=

    \item $J_{\mathrm{institution}}$ — flow of decisions, rules, and governance signals,
    \item $J_{\mathrm{scientific}}$ — flow of models and empirical updates,
    \item $\nabla_i T_8$ — gradients of civilizational tension.
\end{itemize}

A necessary stability condition:

\[
\oint J_{\mathrm{economic}} \, dt \approx 0,
\quad
\oint J_{\mathrm{institution}} \, dt \approx 0.
\]

% ---------------------------------------------------------------
\subsubsection*{6. Cycles \texorpdfstring{$C(M_8)$}{C(M_8)}}

Fundamental cycles of civilization:

\begin{itemize}
    \item $C_{\mathrm{production}}$ — production $\to$ distribution $\to$ consumption $\to$ reinvestment,
    \item $C_{\mathrm{institution}}$ — rule creation $\to$ enforcement $\to$ feedback $\to$ revision,
    \item $C_{\mathrm{infrastructure}}$ — construction $\to$ usage $\to$ maintenance,
    \item $C_{\mathrm{symbol}}$ — encoding $\to$ transmission $\to$ interpretation $\to$ correction,
    \item $C_{\mathrm{science}}$ — hypothesis $\to$ test $\to$ revision $\to$ theory,
    \item $C_{\mathrm{innovation}}$ — invention $\to$ scaling $\to$ saturation $\to$ replacement.
\end{itemize}

A living civilizational continuum requires:

\[
\oint_{C_{\mathrm{symbol}}} dA \approx 0
\quad\text{and}\quad
\oint_{C_{\mathrm{institution}}} dA \approx 0.
\]

% ---------------------------------------------------------------
\subsubsection*{7. Boundary \texorpdfstring{$\partial\Omega(M_8)$}{\partial\Omega(M_8)}}

A system approaches $\partial\Omega(M_8)$ when:

\begin{itemize}
    \item symbolic systems lose fidelity,
    \item infrastructures decay faster than maintenance cycles can compensate,
    \item institutions fail to maintain coherence,
    \item economic circulation collapses,
    \item technological axes fracture (loss of compatibility),
    \item civilizational tension $T_8$ exceeds stability thresholds.
\end{itemize}

Crossing $\partial\Omega(M_8)$ causes reversion to $M_7$-admissible structures.

\subsubsection*{8. Relation to \texorpdfstring{$M_7$}{M_7} and \texorpdfstring{$M_9$}{M_9}}

\textbf{From $M_7$ to $M_8$:}

Necessary conditions:

\[
A_{\mathrm{symbol}} \neq \emptyset, 
\quad 
\tau_{\mathrm{symbol}} \gg \tau_{\mathrm{social}},
\quad
\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{infra}}\ \text{supports macroscopic flows}.
\]

Symbolic stability transforms social meaning into persistent civilization.

\textbf{Toward $M_9$ (theoretical meta-space):}

$M_9$ introduces:

\begin{itemize}
    \item mathematical and scientific theory spaces,
    \item explicit model transformations,
    \item epistemic coherence thresholds,
    \item abstract meta-representations independent of material substrates.
\end{itemize}

Thus $M_8$ provides the material and institutional substrate for $K_9$.
