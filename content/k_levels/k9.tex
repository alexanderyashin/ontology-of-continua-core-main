\subsubsection{\texorpdfstring{$K_9$}{K_9} Overview}
\label{sec:k9-overview}

$K_9$ is the level of \emph{theoretical continua}: large-scale, explicit,
self-referential conceptual systems that structure, predict and integrate
knowledge. Examples include scientific paradigms, formal theories, logical
systems, philosophical frameworks and mathematical structures.

Distinctive features:
\begin{itemize}
    \item explicit symbolic representation,
    \item axiomatic or rule-based organization,
    \item high-level abstraction and model construction,
    \item internal consistency constraints,
    \item meta-level feedback from $K_6$ cognition and $K_8$ civilizations,
    \item long-term paradigmatic cycles (Kuhn-type structure),
    \item cross-theory coupling, unification and competition.
\end{itemize}

$K_9$ is not reducible to $K_8$ culture or $K_6$ cognition; it has its own
dynamical space, thresholds and collapse modes.

\begin{itemize}

    \item contradiction in axioms,
    \item collapse of derivational system,
    \item loss of predictive power,

    \item inability to stabilize symbolic syntax,

Crossing $\partial\Omega(K_9)$ destroys the theory as a continuum.
