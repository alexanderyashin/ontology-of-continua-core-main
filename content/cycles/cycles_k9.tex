\section{Cycles on \texorpdfstring{$K_9$}{K_9}}
\label{sec:cycles-k9}

The level $K_9$ represents the meta-theoretical continuum: the domain of
theories, paradigms, logics, ontologies and methodological frameworks.
Cycles on $K_9$ describe the recurrent structural processes through which
scientific knowledge evolves, stabilises, reorganises and sometimes collapses.

The state space is
\[
  \Omega(K_9)
  =
  \{\text{theories, logics, paradigms, ontologies, models, interpretations}\}.
\]

The axes $A^9(t)$ include:
\[
  A^9 = \{
     \text{logical distinctions},\;
     \text{ontological distinctions},\;
     \text{methodological distinctions},\;
     \text{interpretative dimensions}
  \}.
\]

Potentials $P^9(t)$:
\[
  P^9(t)
  = \{\text{explanatory strength},\; \text{predictive power},\;
        \text{simplicity},\; \text{systematicity},\; \text{coherence}\}.
\]

Flows $J^9(t)$:
\[
  J^9(t) = \{
    \text{argumentation},\;
    \text{proofs},\;
    \text{interpretations},\;
    \text{criticism},\;
    \text{theory transitions}
  \}.
\]

Critical thresholds:
\[
  \Theta^9_\mathrm{logic},\quad
  \Theta^9_\mathrm{emp},\quad
  \Theta^9_\mathrm{heur},\quad
  \Theta^9_\mathrm{axiom}.
\]

A meta-theoretical continuum exists if
\[
  k(K_9)>0,\qquad
  T_9 < \min(\Theta^9).
\]


\subsection{Overview}

Cycles on $K_9$ encode the dynamics of theory formation, stabilization,
evaluation, correction, and revolutionary replacement. They formalise
the systemic processes underlying scientific knowledge and its evolution.
These cycles operate on the structural space $\Omega(K_9)$ and are driven
by logical, empirical and methodological pressures expressed through $T_9$.


\subsection{1. The Normal Science Cycle $C_{\mathrm{norm}}$}

This cycle corresponds to stable periods of theory-driven research.

\[
  \mathrm{Puzzle\;selection}
  \to
  \mathrm{Method\;application}
  \to
  \mathrm{Result\;integration}
  \to
  \mathrm{Coherence\;increase}
  \to
  \mathrm{Puzzle\;selection}.
\]

Formally:
\[
 C_{\mathrm{norm}}
 =
 \left\{
   J^9_{\mathrm{arg}},\;
   P^9_{\mathrm{coherence}},\;
   P^9_{\mathrm{systematicity}}
 \right\},
\]
where $J^9_{\mathrm{arg}}$ is the argumentative flow supporting integration.

Stability requires:
\[
  \Theta^9_\mathrm{logic} < P^9_{\mathrm{coherence}}.
\]


\subsection{2. The Proof–Refutation Cycle $C_{\mathrm{proof/ref}}$}

The foundational logical cycle in science.

\[
  \mathrm{Hypothesis}
  \to
  \mathrm{Derivation}
  \to
  \mathrm{Test}
  \to
  \mathrm{Refutation\;or\;Confirmation}
  \to
  \mathrm{Refinement}
  \to
  \mathrm{Hypothesis}.
\]

Form:
\[
 C_{\mathrm{proof/ref}}
  =
  \left\{
    J^9_{\mathrm{proof}},\;
    J^9_{\mathrm{crit}},\;
    P^9_{\mathrm{predictive}},\;
    \Theta^9_{\mathrm{emp}}
  \right\}.
\]

Threshold:
\[
  P^9_{\mathrm{predictive}} > \Theta^9_\mathrm{emp}.
\]


\subsection{3. The Interpretation Cycle $C_{\mathrm{interp}}$}

Interpretative cycles reorganize theories without altering empirical content.

\[
  \mathrm{Reformulation}
  \to
  \mathrm{Conceptual\;mapping}
  \to
  \mathrm{Reduction\;or\;extension}
  \to
  \mathrm{Unification}
  \to
  \mathrm{Reformulation}.
\]

Form:
\[
 C_{\mathrm{interp}}
  =
  \left\{
    A^9_{\mathrm{interpretation}},\;
    P^9_{\mathrm{simplicity}},\;
    J^9_{\mathrm{reinterpretation}}
  \right\}.
\]

Stability condition:
\[
  P^9_{\mathrm{simplicity}} >
  \Theta^9_{\mathrm{heur}}.
\]


\subsection{4. The Paradigm Cycle $C_{\mathrm{paradigm}}$}

This cycle describes long-term evolution of entire scientific paradigms.

\[
  \mathrm{Model\;development}
  \to
  \mathrm{Problem\;solving}
  \to
  \mathrm{Anomaly\;accumulation}
  \to
  \mathrm{Crisis}
  \to
  \mathrm{Reconstruction}
  \to
  \mathrm{Model\;development}.
\]

Form:
\[
 C_{\mathrm{paradigm}}
  =
  \left\{
    P^9_{\mathrm{explanatory}},\;
    J^9_{\mathrm{crit}},\;
    A^9_{\mathrm{ontology}},\;
    \Theta^9_{\mathrm{axiom}}
  \right\}.
\]

Threshold (Kuhn-type):
\[
  T_9 > \Theta^9_\mathrm{logic}
  \quad\Rightarrow\quad
  \text{Paradigm instability}.
\]


\subsection{5. The Scientific Revolution Cycle $C_{\mathrm{rev}}$}

The rapid, high-tension transition where a new paradigm replaces the old.

\[
  \mathrm{Crisis}
  \to
  \mathrm{Competing\;frameworks}
  \to
  \mathrm{Selection}
  \to
  \mathrm{Reinterpretation\;of\;past}
  \to
  \mathrm{Stabilisation}
  \to
  \mathrm{Crisis}.
\]

Formally:
\[
 C_{\mathrm{rev}}
   =
   \left\{
     J^9_{\mathrm{transition}},\;
     A^9_{\mathrm{methodology}},\;
     P^9_{\mathrm{explanatory}},\;
     P^9_{\mathrm{predictive}}
   \right\}.
\]

The transition condition (meta-theoretical phase transition):
\[
  T_9 = \Theta^9_{\mathrm{dim}}.
\]

Cycle reinstates a new structure of $\Omega(K_9)$.

\[
  \mathrm{Axiom\;selection}
  \to
  \mathrm{Derivation}
  \to
  \mathrm{Consistency\;check}
  \to
  \mathrm{Revision}
  \to
  \mathrm{Axiom\;selection}.

Form:
\[
 C_{\mathrm{logic}}
  =
  \left\{
    A^9_{\mathrm{logic}},\;
    \Theta^9_{\mathrm{axiom}},\;
    J^9_{\mathrm{proof}}
  \right\}.
\]

Linked to Gödel-type limits:
\[
  P^9_{\mathrm{coherence}} > \Theta^9_{\mathrm{axiom}}
  \;\;\text{for viability}.
\]

\subsection{Collapse of \texorpdfstring{$K_9$}{K_9} Cycles}

Collapse occurs when:

\[
  P^9_{\mathrm{coherence}} < \Theta^9_{\mathrm{logic}},\qquad
  P^9_{\mathrm{predictive}} < \Theta^9_{\mathrm{emp}},\qquad
  P^9_{\mathrm{simplicity}} < \Theta^9_{\mathrm{heur}}.
\]

Then:

\[
  C_j \to \varnothing,\qquad
  \Omega(K_9)=\varnothing,\qquad
  k(K_9)\to 0.
\]

This corresponds to the death of a paradigm or meta-framework.

\[
