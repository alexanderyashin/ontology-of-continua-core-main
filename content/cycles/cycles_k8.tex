\subsubsection{Cycles on \texorpdfstring{$K_8$}{K_8}}
\label{sec:cycles-k8}

The level $K_8$ represents the civilizational continuum: a large-scale,
multi-component system integrating population, infrastructures, energy base,
logistics, information systems and economic mechanisms of reproduction.
Cycles on $K_8$ formalise the recurrent macro-processes that allow a
civilization to sustain itself over historical time.

The state of $K_8$ is:
\[
  s(t) = (\text{Pop}(t), \text{Infra}(t), \text{Tech}(t),
           \text{Energy}(t), \text{Info}(t), \text{Econ}(t)).
\]

The fundamental flows (memory~\#28) are:
\[
  J^{(8)} =
  (J_{\mathrm{energy}},\,
   J_{\mathrm{material}},\,
   J_{\mathrm{logistics}},\,
   J_{\mathrm{info}},\,
   J_{\mathrm{population}}).
\]

Critical thresholds:
\[
  \Theta_E,\;
  \Theta_L,\;
  \Theta_I,\;
  \Theta_M,\;
  \Theta_R.
\]

Structural tension:
\[
  T_8 = 
    w_E(E_{\mathrm{crit}} - E_{\mathrm{density}})
  + w_L(L_{\mathrm{demand}} - L_{\mathrm{capacity}})
  + w_M(M_{\mathrm{demand}} - M_{\mathrm{repair}})
  + w_I(I_{\mathrm{complexity}} - I_{\mathrm{coherence}})
  + w_R(R_{\mathrm{demand}} - R_{\mathrm{supply}}).
\]

The existence of a civilizational continuum requires:
\[
  T_8 < 0,\qquad k_8>0.
\]

\subsubsection{Overview}

Civilizational cycles describe the macro-recurrent processes through which
societies maintain material reproduction, energy flows, knowledge coherence,
logistics, infrastructure sustainability and demographic stability.  
These cycles integrate the dynamic interactions between subsystems and ensure
the persistence of $\Omega(K_8)$.

\subsubsection{1. Energy Reproduction Cycle $C_{\mathrm{energy}}$}

Energy density is the primary determinant of civilizational viability.

\[
  \mathrm{Extraction}
  \to
  \mathrm{Conversion}
  \to
  \mathrm{Distribution}
  \to
  \mathrm{Use}
  \to
  \mathrm{Maintenance}
  \to
  \mathrm{Extraction}.
\]

Formally:
\[
  C_{\mathrm{energy}} =
  \left\{
    J_{\mathrm{energy}}(t),\;
    E_{\mathrm{density}}(t),\;
    \mathrm{Infra}_{\mathrm{energy}}(t)
  \right\}.
\]

Threshold:
\[
  E_{\mathrm{density}} > \Theta_E.
\]

  \to

  \to
  \mathrm{Waste\;processing}
  \to

\subsubsection{3. Infrastructure Maintenance Cycle $C_{\mathrm{infra}}$}

Civilizational stability depends on maintaining complex infrastructures.

\[
  \mathrm{Inspection}
  \to
  \mathrm{Repair}
  \to
  \mathrm{Upgrade}
  \to
  \mathrm{Integration}
  \to
  \mathrm{Inspection}.

Form:
\[
  C_{\mathrm{infra}} =
  \left\{
     M_{\mathrm{repair}}(t),\;

  \right\}.
\]

Threshold:
\[
  M_{\mathrm{repair}} > \Theta_M.
\]

\subsubsection{4. Information Coherence Cycle $C_{\mathrm{info}}$}

A civilization requires consistent information-processing capacity.

\[
  \mathrm{Generation}
  \to
  \mathrm{Transmission}
  \to
  \mathrm{Integration}
  \to
  \mathrm{Coherence\;checking}
  \to
  \mathrm{Update}

\[
 C_{\mathrm{info}}

 \left\{

Threshold:
\[
  I_{\mathrm{coherence}} > \Theta_I.
\]

\subsubsection{5. Demographic Reproduction Cycle $C_{\mathrm{pop}}$}

Demographic stability is necessary for sustaining labor, knowledge transfer,
military capacity and intergenerational reproduction.

\[
  \mathrm{Birth}
  \to
  \mathrm{Growth}
  \to
  \mathrm{Education}
  \to
  \mathrm{Labour}
  \to
  \mathrm{Aging}
  \to
  \mathrm{Birth}.
\]

Form:
\[
  C_{\mathrm{pop}}
    =
    \left\{
      J_{\mathrm{population}}(t),\;
      R_{\mathrm{supply}}(t),\;
      R_{\mathrm{demand}}(t)
    \right\}.
\]

Threshold:
\[
  R_{\mathrm{supply}} > \Theta_R.
\]

\subsubsection{6. Macro-Economic Reproduction Cycle $C_{\mathrm{econ}}$}

The economic cycle:

\[
  \mathrm{Investment}
  \to
  \mathrm{Production}
  \to
  \mathrm{Distribution}
  \to
  \mathrm{Consumption}
  \to
  \mathrm{Reinvestment}.
\]

Formally:
\[
 C_{\mathrm{econ}}=
 \left\{
   \mathrm{Econ}(t),\;
   J_{\mathrm{material}}(t),\;
   J_{\mathrm{energy}}(t),\;
   J_{\mathrm{info}}(t)
 \right\}.
\]

Stability requires:
\[
  T_{\mathrm{econ}} < \min(\Theta_L, \Theta_E).
\]

\subsubsection{7. Integrated Civilizational Cycle $C_{\mathrm{civ}}$}

The civilizational continuum persists only if all major reproduction cycles
remain within stable thresholds.

\[
 C_{\mathrm{civ}}
   =
     C_{\mathrm{energy}}
     \cup C_{\mathrm{mat/log}}
     \cup C_{\mathrm{infra}}
     \cup C_{\mathrm{info}}
     \cup C_{\mathrm{pop}}
     \cup C_{\mathrm{econ}}.
\]

Stability requires:
\[
  T_8
   <
   \min(\Theta_E,\Theta_L,\Theta_I,\Theta_M,\Theta_R).
\]

If any subthreshold fails, $\Omega(K_8)$ undergoes collapse.

\subsubsection{Cycle Metrics on \texorpdfstring{$K_8$}{K_8}}

\paragraph{Cycle length.}
\[
  L(C) = \oint d\Omega(K_8).
\]

\paragraph{Efficiency.}
\[
  C_{\mathrm{eff}}
  = \frac{\oint J^{(8)}\cdot dA}{L(C)}.
\]

\paragraph{Stability.}
\[
  S(C)
    = \min_{t\in C}
      d(\Omega(K_8),\partial\Omega(K_8)).
\]

\paragraph{Weight.}
\[
 w(C)
 =
 F(C_{\mathrm{eff}},
   S(C),
   E_{\mathrm{density}},
   L_{\mathrm{capacity}},
   I_{\mathrm{coherence}}).
\]

\subsubsection{Collapse of \texorpdfstring{$K_8$}{K_8} Cycles}

Collapse occurs when:

\[
  E_{\mathrm{density}} < \Theta_E,\qquad
  L_{\mathrm{capacity}} < \Theta_L,\qquad
  I_{\mathrm{coherence}} < \Theta_I,\qquad
  M_{\mathrm{repair}} < \Theta_M,\qquad
  R_{\mathrm{supply}} < \Theta_R.
\]

Under collapse:

\[
  C_j \to \varnothing,\qquad
  \Omega(K_8)=\varnothing,\qquad
  k_8\to 0.
\]

This corresponds to full civilizational breakdown.

\subsubsection{Continuity from \texorpdfstring{$K_7$}{K_7} to \texorpdfstring{$K_8$}{K_8}}

The transition $K_7\to K_8$ occurs when:

\[
  T_{\mathrm{soc}} > \Theta_{\mathrm{dim}},
\]

and new axes appear:

\[
  A_{\mathrm{energy}},\;
  A_{\mathrm{logistics}},\;
  A_{\mathrm{infra}},\;
  A_{\mathrm{population}},\;
  A_{\mathrm{econ}}.
\]

This introduces macro-reproduction cycles with no analogue at the social level
$K_7$.
